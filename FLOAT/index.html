We realised we were designing and imagining a next economy for small towns. A way of living in regional places that could derive its income from its real value. We have extraordinary local knowledge. We paddle our kayaks. We are better at observing the weather cos it determines our journeys. We navigate more and more by nature. We are stronger. We are fitter. We instinctively look out for others. We bring food to share. We paddle to the FLOAT and clamber aboard for an artists talk every now and then. We own our solutions. A school of untourism will embody what weve learned. For public consumption by travellers, who are looking for more than motels and lattes. Its time to launch our School of Untourism an experimental creative adventure @ Lake Tyers. FLOAT COLLECTIVE

A COMMUNIVERSITY. AN OBSERVATORIUM. A CREATIVE (ECOLOGY/ECONOM)Y

FLOAT.

A floating arts residency on Lake Tyers. East Gippsland. Weve been working away at this for a few years now. As a community of like-minds. Adventurous, experimental artists and passionate scientists and citizen scientists.

FLOAT. We designed it. We built it. We operate it. We share it. We look after it. And we learn a lot about the Lake as we do it. Artists come. From all over the place. They taste us. They stay. They share their work here, and to a global audience. They come back. They bring friends. Over and over.

OBSERVATORIUM. We know this is a really important job for us here on the Gippsland Lakes. We paddle. We sit. We notice things. We gather sounds. We record the bats in the trees around us. We hear the fish jumping underneath us. We NOTICE THE WEATHER. We noticed when the lake got so low that we all held our breath for rain. Now we wait for the berm to break.

THE VESSEL. We fret about greywater, compost loos, systems, freshwater, algae, jetties and lifejackets. We repair. We maintain. We plan the next big thing. Induct the next artist. Clean and prepare the vessel and then paddle them to their extraordinary residence. And we tow their belongings.

Yes we still get overwhelmed at times with the job of doing it all but theres a much bigger thing going on here. And we rely on the artists who come to stay, to understand that its not really about them. Or us. Its life as we know it. And its shaping a future for what art can be in regional communities.

ECONOMICS
Theres such a long history of art being given away. We need a new economics. So, its time to think harder about the economics of real life, real value, real jobs, real tourism and all the things we long for, in our far away places, to provide happy balanced lives for the good people who live here. And those who want to live here. An economics that shifts the value of things. For us that includes valuing knowledge and making that knowledge a career path, turning it into income but not in the usual way.

FLOAT
Central to FLOATs philosophy is a truly valuable role for art in leading change. We broadcast our role through residencies, commissions, conversation, curating, making, writing and activism within the Lake Tyers catchment. And while many bits appear frustratingly organic, as we find our way, some of us see chaos as necessarily preceding significant change. To demonstrate that art is life.

That art is a legitmate sector of the business economy.
That art communicates a love of environment through shared knowledge. To articulate and resource a new network model for creative impact in East Gippsland. To create a hybrid model of arts engagment, education and collaborativedelivery that maximises the bang of every dollar spent on community To expand the FLOAT residency into a Gippsland Lakes culture of creative observation. To transform the way we curate and communicate a public appreciation of regional communities as exciting, generous and thoughful destinations.
To showcase communities that build on deep local knowledge as our greatest asset.
To nurture a constant exchange between urban and rural.
To become an acknowledged contributor to regional recovery.
